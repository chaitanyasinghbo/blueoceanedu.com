# Blue Ocean Education — Product Context

## Product Purpose

Landing page and primary web presence for Blue Ocean Education, India's premier student talent-development and admissions advisory. Design IS the product here: the site must communicate quality, selectivity, and genuine differentiation before a visitor reads a single word.

## Register

Brand. This is a landing page where design IS the product.

## Users

**Primary:** Parents of ambitious Indian students in Grades 8 to 12, living in South Delhi, Gurgaon, Mumbai, Bangalore. Late evening browsing on MacBook. Warm ambient light. Has already seen 3 to 4 generic consultancies. Searching for something that feels genuinely different: serious, bespoke, credible. Willing to invest Rs 5 to 10 lakh if they trust the quality.

**Secondary:** Indian professionals aged 22 to 32 researching MBA/Masters admissions.

## Brand Voice

Three words: earned, rigorous, rare.

Physical object: a letter from a Harvard admissions office. Authoritative, precise, warm. Never boastful or corporate.

Tone rules: no em dashes, no AI filler, no "holistic journey," no star ratings, no template copy.

## Anti-References

- Every other Indian education/admissions website (bright edtech blue, happy stock photos, hollow promises)
- Generic SaaS landing pages
- Finance/insurance sites dressed up as premium
- JEE coaching aesthetics

## Positive References

- Aman Hotels website (extreme premium, editorial, photography-forward, restraint as voice)
- Sotheby's (institutional authority, typographic confidence, serious)
- A serious academic press catalogue (Harvard University Press, MIT Press)

## Strategic Principles

1. The visitor should feel they have found something genuinely different within 3 seconds
2. The design should signal selection and scarcity, not volume and urgency
3. Data and proof matter but must never feel like a SaaS metrics dashboard
4. The philosophy section is as important as the results section
5. The right students should self-qualify; the wrong students should self-disqualify

## Typography

Display: EB Garamond (serif, Google Fonts, wght 400-800 variable). Italic for hero and key emotional moments.
Body/UI: Bricolage Grotesque (variable sans, Google Fonts).

## Color Strategy

Committed. Warm cream ground (oklch 96.5% 0.01 78) for most surfaces. Deep teal (oklch 22% 0.07 195) for brand sections: manifesto, profile architecture, CTA, footer. Amber accent (oklch 72% 0.14 75) used rarely for one or two key callouts only.
